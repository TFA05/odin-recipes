Grilled Sandwiches

Description

This is a delicious grilled chicken panini recipe that is fast and easy to make. Use boneless, skinless chicken breasts for lean protein that is well-seasoned and scented with rosemary and garlic. The chicken breasts can be grilled ahead of time and then used to make slices for sandwiches for the next couple of days. As well, the grilled chicken can also be added to salads for another quick and healthy dinner or lunch.

As for the sandwich itself, you may have a panini maker or George Foreman grill that you like to use. But you can also use the old-school method and press it right there on your gas or charcoal grill. While you could simply grill it without pressing it, for a real panini feel you need a weight. There are cast iron tools such as a grill press or bacon press that work well for this, sitting on top of the sandwich. But an equally effective tool is a clean brick wrapped in a couple of layers of aluminum foil. You will need oven mitts to manipulate the hot weight or brick.

Ingredients

  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil, plus more for the pan
  • 2 teaspoons fresh rosemary, chopped
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 4 tablespoons Italian dressing
  • 8 slices Italian bread
  • 1 tomato, sliced
  • 8 ounces mozzarella cheese, sliced

Steps

  1. Gather the ingredients.
  2. Preheat grill for medium-high heat.
  3. Brush the chicken with olive oil and season with chopped rosemary, garlic powder, salt, and pepper
  4. Lightly oil the grill grates.
  5. Place the chicken on the grill and cook for 7 minutes per side or until juices run clear and chicken is no longer pink.
  6. Remove the chicken from the grill. Allow it to cool for 5 to 10 minutes.
  7. Cut it into 1/2-inch slices.
  8. Drizzle Italian dressing on 4 slices of bread and on chicken pieces.
  9. Add tomato and cheese and sliced chicken breasts to the bread.
  10. Cover with the remaining 4 slices of bread and brush with olive oil
  11. Over medium heat, place the sandwiches on the grill. Place the weight on top of the sandwich and cook the panini for 4 minutes.
  12. Remove the weight, turn the sandwiches over, and replace the weight. Cook for 4 minutes on this side, or until the cheese melts.
  13. Remove from heat and serve with your favorite side of antipasto salad, potato salad, macaroni salad, chips, or pickle.
